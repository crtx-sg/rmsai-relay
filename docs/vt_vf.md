# Ventricular Tachycardia and Ventricular Fibrillation

## Ventricular fibrillation
Ventricular fibrillation is a life-threatening, disorganized ventricular rhythm with no effective
cardiac output. It is a cardiac arrest rhythm. Begin the ACLS protocol immediately: start
high-quality CPR and deliver an unsynchronized defibrillation shock as soon as a defibrillator is
available.

## Pulseless ventricular tachycardia
Pulseless ventricular tachycardia is treated identically to ventricular fibrillation: immediate
CPR and defibrillation. Administer epinephrine every 3 to 5 minutes and consider amiodarone for
shock-refractory rhythms.

## Stable ventricular tachycardia
A patient in ventricular tachycardia who still has a pulse and stable blood pressure may be
treated with intravenous antiarrhythmics such as amiodarone. If the patient becomes unstable,
perform synchronized cardioversion.

## Post-resuscitation
After return of spontaneous circulation, provide targeted temperature management, treat reversible
causes, and arrange continuous monitoring in a critical care setting.
