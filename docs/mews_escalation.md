# Modified Early Warning Score (MEWS) and Escalation

## Purpose
The Modified Early Warning Score is a bedside tool that aggregates physiological observations into
a single score to flag clinical deterioration early. Components include heart rate, systolic blood
pressure, respiratory rate, temperature, and oxygen saturation.

## Scoring
Each component contributes 0 to 3 points based on how far it deviates from the normal range. The
total score determines the risk level: a total of 0 to 1 is low risk, 2 to 3 is medium risk, 4 to
5 is high risk, and 6 or more is critical risk.

## Escalation thresholds
A score of 4 or more should trigger urgent bedside review by the responsible clinician. A score of
6 or more, or any single component scoring 3, should prompt immediate escalation to the rapid
response or critical care outreach team.

## Trend monitoring
A rising trend matters as much as the absolute value. If the MEWS increases by 2 or more points
between observations, escalate to the on-call clinician within 30 minutes even if the absolute
score is not yet critical.
